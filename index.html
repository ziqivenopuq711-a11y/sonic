Sonic Labs and Immunefi Launch $2M Bug Bounty Program to Fortify Blockchain Security

December 31, 2024 | Crypto Security | bug-bounty  immunefi  sonic-labs

Sonic Labs, the team behind the high-performance Sonic blockchain (formerly Fantom), has teamed up with Immunefi — the leading Web3 bug bounty platform — to launch a $2 million bug bounty program aimed at fortifying the security of both the Sonic L1 protocol and the Gateway bridge. The Gateway bridge connects Sonic to Ethereum, enabling seamless transfer of EVM assets including USDC and Wrapped Ether. The program signals a major step forward in Sonic's security posture as it prepares for broader DeFi adoption.

Sonic Labs previously secured audit reviews from top firms including OpenZeppelin, Quantstamp, and Certora. The bug bounty program complements these formal audits by inviting the global security research community to test the protocol under real-world conditions. Immunefi has paid out over $110 million to security researchers across the industry, establishing itself as the gold standard for on-chain vulnerability disclosure and responsible security research.

The Sonic blockchain is engineered for high-performance DeFi applications, supporting up to 10,000 transactions per second with finality in under 720 milliseconds — performance metrics that significantly exceed Ethereum's native Layer-1 throughput. As Sonic scales its ecosystem and attracts greater TVL, the security of both the base layer and the bridge infrastructure becomes increasingly critical. The $2M bug bounty program represents Sonic Labs' commitment to maintaining the highest security standards as the network matures.

Key Questions & Takeaways

What is the purpose of the bug bounty program launched by Sonic Labs and Immunefi?

The purpose is to enhance the security of Sonic's L1 protocol and Gateway bridge by rewarding security researchers for responsibly identifying and disclosing vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.

How does the Gateway bridge function in relation to Sonic and Ethereum?

The Gateway bridge connects Sonic to the Ethereum network, allowing users to transfer EVM-compatible assets like USDC and Wrapped Ether between the two networks, enabling interoperability and capital flow across ecosystems.

Who supports the Fantom to Sonic token migration?

Several major exchanges, including Binance, support the 1:1 Fantom to Sonic token migration, providing liquidity infrastructure for users transitioning between the legacy Fantom ecosystem and the new Sonic blockchain.

What other projects are currently featured on Immunefi's bug bounty platform?

Immunefi hosts bug bounty programs for Ethereum, Stacks, Lombard, and dozens of other major blockchain protocols, with significant rewards available for identifying vulnerabilities — contributing to the platform's $110M+ total payout record.

What steps has Sonic Labs taken to ensure the security of their network?

Sonic Labs has engaged top audit firms OpenZeppelin, Quantstamp, and Certora for formal security reviews; partnered with Immunefi to launch a $2M bug bounty program; and established ongoing security collaboration with key industry players to maintain network integrity.
