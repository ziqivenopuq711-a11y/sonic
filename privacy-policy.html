SonicLabsNews Privacy Policy: Data Protection & User Rights

Information Collection & How We Use Your Data

SonicLabsNews is committed to maintaining the trust of our readers through transparent, minimal data collection practices that respect user privacy while enabling us to deliver the high-quality cryptocurrency news and analysis our audience relies upon. This privacy policy describes in clear, accessible language exactly what information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, and the controls available to users who wish to manage their data or exercise their privacy rights. The primary personal data we collect is email addresses voluntarily provided by readers who subscribe to our daily newsletter or market alert services — this information is used exclusively to deliver the requested communications and is never sold or shared with third parties without explicit consent.

Our website analytics systems collect anonymized usage data including pages visited, session duration, referral sources, and general geographic region (country-level only) to help us understand content performance and optimize our editorial strategy — this data is processed in aggregate and is not linked to individual user identities. When readers contact us through our inquiry forms, we collect the information provided — name, email address, message content, and any attachments — for the sole purpose of responding to the inquiry, and we retain this correspondence only as long as necessary for the business purpose that prompted the contact. We do not collect financial information, cryptocurrency wallet addresses, or trading activity data from our readers through our website.

Cookies, Tracking Technologies & Your Control Options

SonicLabsNews uses cookies and related tracking technologies to provide core website functionality, understand how readers use our platform, and — where consent is provided — to enable relevant content recommendations. We categorize the cookies we use into three groups based on their purpose and the consent basis under which they are deployed. Essential cookies are strictly necessary for basic website operation: they enable session management, security features, and core navigation functionality, and cannot be disabled without breaking basic site functionality — these do not require consent under applicable privacy law.

Analytics cookies, deployed through Google Analytics 4, collect anonymized behavioral data about how visitors interact with our content — which articles are read, how long readers spend on pages, what navigation paths are taken — helping us understand what coverage our audience finds most valuable and optimize our editorial product accordingly. These cookies are deployed only where applicable law permits or where users have not opted out via browser-level Do Not Track signals, which our systems honor. Marketing and personalization cookies, if deployed, enable features like related article recommendations and returning visitor recognition — these are always consent-dependent and are never activated without affirmative opt-in where required by applicable law. Users can manage cookie preferences at any time through their browser settings or through the cookie preference center accessible via our website footer.

Third-Party Services, Integrations & External Links

SonicLabsNews integrates with several carefully selected third-party services to deliver core functionality to our platform, and we believe transparency about these integrations is essential to maintaining reader trust. Email delivery and newsletter management is handled through industry-standard email service providers that process subscriber data under strict data processing agreements that obligate them to handle personal information solely for the purpose of delivering our communications and prohibit secondary use of our subscriber data for their own marketing purposes. Web analytics are provided by Google Analytics, which processes usage data under Google’s data processing terms — readers who prefer not to participate in Google Analytics can install the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on or use privacy-focused browsers or extensions.

Our content delivery network (CDN) services are provided by Cloudflare, which processes connection metadata (IP addresses, request headers) to provide DDoS protection and accelerated content delivery — this processing occurs under Cloudflare’s privacy policy and is inherent to the functioning of the CDN service. Embedded content on our platform — trading charts from TradingView, price data widgets from CoinGecko, video content from YouTube — loads resources from those providers’ servers and is subject to their respective privacy policies. Our website contains links to external sites including protocol documentation, exchange platforms, regulatory resources, and research papers, and we have no control over the privacy practices of those external sites; we recommend readers review privacy policies of any external sites they visit.

Your Privacy Rights & How to Exercise Them

SonicLabsNews is committed to honoring the privacy rights of all our users, including the expanded rights available to residents of jurisdictions with comprehensive data protection legislation such as the European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California’s Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and similar frameworks that are emerging globally. Under these frameworks, our users have the right to access the personal data we hold about them — submitting a data access request will result in delivery of a comprehensive summary of all personal data we have collected and processed within our systems, typically within 30 days of the verified request.

The right to rectification allows users to request correction of inaccurate personal data — if we have incorrect information about you in our systems, we will correct it promptly upon receiving a substantiated correction request. The right to erasure (the “right to be forgotten”) enables users to request deletion of their personal data from our systems; we will comply with all valid deletion requests within 30 days, subject to narrow exceptions where retention is required by applicable law. Data portability rights entitle users to receive their personal data in a structured, machine-readable format for transfer to another service. Opt-out rights under CCPA allow California residents to direct us not to sell their personal information — we do not sell personal information, but we honor this right as a matter of principle. Children’s privacy is protected through our policy of not knowingly collecting personal data from individuals under the age of 16.

Security Measures, Policy Updates & Our Accountability Commitment

SonicLabsNews implements industry-standard security measures across our entire technical infrastructure to protect the personal data entrusted to us by our readers against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. All data transmitted between users’ browsers and our web servers is encrypted using current TLS protocols, ensuring that connection content cannot be intercepted by network-level observers. Our systems undergo regular security assessments and penetration testing conducted by qualified external security firms, with findings remediated according to risk-prioritized schedules. Access to personal data within our organization is restricted on a need-to-know basis, with role-based access controls ensuring that only team members with legitimate operational need can access reader data.

In the event of a data security incident that poses a meaningful risk to user rights and freedoms, we are committed to notifying affected users within 72 hours of becoming aware of the incident — a standard that exceeds the requirements of many applicable privacy regulations and reflects our belief that prompt, transparent communication is the appropriate response to security failures. We reserve the right to update this privacy policy as our data practices evolve, applicable law changes, or new services are added to our platform; all material changes will be communicated to newsletter subscribers via email with at least 14 days advance notice before the updated policy takes effect, and the current version of the policy will always be accessible through our website. Questions about this privacy policy, requests to exercise privacy rights, or concerns about our data practices can be directed to hello@sonicnewslabs.com.
