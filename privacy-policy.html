Privacy Policy

Introduction

This Privacy Policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and use of personal information by EdgexNews, the digital media platform operated at this domain (collectively referred to as "EdgexNews," "we," "our," or "us"). This policy is effective as of April 1, 2026, and applies to all visitors, readers, newsletter subscribers, press release submitters, and any other individuals who interact with the EdgexNews platform through any means, including direct website visits, email correspondence, social media interactions, and third-party referral links that direct users to our content. By continuing to use the EdgexNews platform after the effective date of this policy, you acknowledge that you have read and understood its terms and agree to the collection and use of your information as described herein.

EdgexNews is committed to handling personal data with the highest standards of care, transparency, and compliance with applicable data protection legislation. We recognize that the privacy and security of our readers' personal information is a matter of significant trust, and we have designed our data practices to collect only the information genuinely necessary to operate and improve our platform, to use that information only in the ways described in this policy, and to protect it against unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse through appropriate technical and organizational security measures. We do not sell personal data to third parties, we do not engage in targeted advertising based on individual browsing profiles, and we do not share personal information with external parties except in the limited circumstances described in this policy.

Data We Collect

EdgexNews collects several categories of information as part of normal platform operation. Technical data collected automatically when you visit our platform includes your IP address (collected in anonymized or pseudonymized form), browser type and version, operating system, screen resolution, referring URL, pages visited within our platform, time spent on individual pages, and the date and time of your visit. This technical data is captured through server log files and analytics software and is used solely for the purposes of understanding how our platform is used, identifying technical issues, measuring content performance, and improving the overall user experience. We do not associate this technical data with personally identifiable information except where you have explicitly provided such information through a form or subscription interaction.

When you voluntarily submit information to EdgexNews — for example, by subscribing to our email newsletter, submitting a press release, or contacting us through our editorial or press inquiry channels — we collect the personal information you provide, which may include your name, email address, company affiliation, job title, and the content of your message or submission. Newsletter subscription data is used exclusively to deliver the newsletter and related communications to which you have subscribed, and is never shared with third parties for any purpose, including advertising. Press release submissions may include information about organizations and named individuals that EdgexNews may reference in published content in accordance with standard journalistic practice for company-issued announcements. We retain personal data collected through voluntary submissions for no longer than necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected.

How We Use Your Data

The personal and technical data collected by EdgexNews is used for a specific and limited set of purposes that are described in full in this section. Automatically collected technical data is used to operate and maintain the platform, diagnose and resolve technical errors, analyze aggregate readership patterns to inform editorial decisions about content relevance and format, and generate anonymized statistical reports about platform performance that may be shared publicly or used in commercial discussions with advertising partners. This aggregate and anonymized data does not contain any information that could be used to identify individual users and poses no privacy risk to our readers.

Personal information voluntarily provided through newsletter subscriptions is used solely to deliver the newsletter content and any transactional communications directly related to your subscription, such as confirmation emails, notification of subscription changes, and account-related alerts. We do not use newsletter subscriber data to build advertising profiles, we do not share subscriber lists with third parties, and we do not send newsletter subscribers communications unrelated to the EdgexNews newsletter service without obtaining separate explicit consent. Personal information provided through editorial or press inquiries is used to respond to your communication and, where relevant, to support the editorial work product that resulted from your contact. EdgexNews does not sell, license, rent, or otherwise transfer personal data to any third party for commercial purposes under any circumstances.

Cookies and Tracking

EdgexNews uses cookies and similar tracking technologies as part of normal website operation. Cookies are small text files placed on your device by web servers that enable websites to recognize returning visitors, maintain session state, measure user engagement, and deliver functionality that depends on persistent state across page loads. EdgexNews uses several categories of cookies: strictly necessary cookies that are essential to platform functionality and cannot be disabled without impairing your ability to use the site; performance and analytics cookies that collect anonymized data about how visitors interact with our content to help us improve the platform; and functionality cookies that remember your preferences, such as reading mode settings, to provide a more personalized experience on return visits.

We use third-party analytics services, including industry-standard web analytics platforms, to help us understand aggregate patterns in how our content is consumed. These services may set their own cookies on your device and may collect data about your visits to our platform in accordance with their own privacy policies. EdgexNews does not permit third-party analytics providers to use data collected on our platform for their own purposes beyond providing the analytics service to us. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels that build behavioral profiles for targeted advertising purposes. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings, which allow you to view, delete, and block cookies from specific websites. Note that disabling certain cookie categories may impair the functionality of some features of the EdgexNews platform. A cookie preference management interface is available via the privacy settings link in our page footer.

Your Rights

Individuals whose personal data is processed by EdgexNews are entitled to a range of rights under applicable data protection legislation, including the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as well as equivalent legislation in other jurisdictions. These rights include the right to access the personal data we hold about you and to receive it in a portable, machine-readable format; the right to request correction of inaccurate personal data; the right to request deletion of personal data we hold about you, subject to any overriding legal obligations we may have to retain certain information; the right to object to or restrict certain categories of processing; and, where processing is based on consent, the right to withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

To exercise any of the rights described in this section, or to submit a complaint about our data practices, please contact us at editorial@edgexnews.com with the subject line "Privacy Request." We will acknowledge receipt of your request within 72 hours and will fulfill substantive requests within the timeframes required by applicable law — typically 30 days for GDPR requests and 45 days for CCPA requests, with the possibility of a single extension where the complexity or volume of requests makes it necessary. California residents have the right to request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, the categories of sources from which it was collected, the business or commercial purposes for which it is used, and the categories of third parties with whom it is shared. EdgexNews does not sell personal information as defined under the CCPA, and California residents are therefore not required to submit an opt-out request for the sale of personal data, as no such sale takes place.
