Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

Welcome to AvantisNews. This Privacy Policy explains how AvantisNews Media Ltd ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you visit our website at avantisnews.com (the "Site") or interact with any of our digital services. We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information in a transparent, responsible manner consistent with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable.

By accessing or using the Site, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this policy, please discontinue use of the Site. We reserve the right to amend this policy at any time, and material changes will be communicated through a prominent notice on our homepage or, where required by law, via direct notification to affected users.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect several types of information when you visit and interact with AvantisNews:

  • Information you provide directly: This includes your name, email address, and any other details you submit when subscribing to our newsletter, completing a contact form, submitting a press release, or registering for any account-based features we may offer.
  • Usage data: We automatically collect information about your interactions with the Site, including the pages you visit, the time and duration of your visit, your referring URL, browser type, device type, operating system, and IP address. This data is collected using industry-standard logging technologies and third-party analytics platforms.
  • Cookies and tracking technologies: We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze Site performance, and serve relevant content. You may adjust your browser settings to reject cookies, though doing so may affect certain Site functionality. A detailed cookie preference center is accessible via the cookie notice displayed on your first visit.

3. How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is used for the following purposes:

  • To deliver the content, features, and services you request, including newsletter delivery and press release distribution.
  • To analyze website traffic and user behavior in order to improve the quality, relevance, and performance of our editorial content and site features.
  • To respond to your inquiries, process submissions, and provide customer support.
  • To send you marketing communications, updates, and promotional materials, where you have provided explicit consent or where we have a legitimate interest to do so. You may opt out of marketing communications at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any email or by contacting us directly.
  • To comply with applicable legal obligations, enforce our Terms of Service, and protect the rights, property, and safety of AvantisNews and our users.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their independent marketing purposes. We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We engage trusted third-party companies to assist with email delivery, analytics, hosting, and payment processing. These providers are contractually obligated to process your data solely in accordance with our instructions and applicable data protection standards.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose personal information if required to do so by law, court order, or in response to a valid request from a government authority, or where we believe disclosure is necessary to protect our legal rights or prevent harm.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of our assets, user data may be transferred to the acquiring entity, subject to the same protections described in this policy.

5. Your Rights and How to Exercise Them

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights with respect to your personal information: the right to access the data we hold about you; the right to rectify inaccurate data; the right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") where retention is no longer necessary or lawful; the right to restrict processing; the right to data portability; and the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. To exercise any of these rights, please submit a request through our Contact page. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law, and in all cases within 30 days of receiving a verified request. Please note that some requests may be subject to legal limitations or identity verification requirements before we can fulfill them.
