Bitcoin ETF Products Record $1.2 Billion in Inflows During a Single Trading Week — Highest Since January Approval

Spot Bitcoin ETFs listed on US exchanges collectively absorbed over $1.2 billion in net new capital during a five-day stretch in late April, surpassing the previous weekly record set during the initial approval euphoria. BlackRock's IBIT alone accounted for $680 million of those flows, reinforcing its position as the dominant vehicle for institutional Bitcoin exposure. Analysts at several prime brokerage desks attribute the surge to pension fund advisors completing due diligence and receiving internal investment committee approvals, signaling that the institutionalization of Bitcoin as a treasury asset is accelerating well beyond early adopters.

Market Overview

The broader cryptocurrency market has staged a convincing recovery throughout April 2026, with total market capitalization climbing from $2.1 trillion to $2.6 trillion over the month. Bitcoin led the charge, retesting the $70,000 psychological level multiple times before consolidating in the $66,000–$69,000 band. Ethereum outperformed on a percentage basis following the activation of the EIP-7623 blob cost recalibration, which further reduced Layer-2 transaction fees and reignited developer activity on protocols built on the Ethereum settlement layer.

The altcoin market produced several standout performers. Solana's native token surged 38% after the network processed a record 85 million transactions in a single day without measurable congestion, validating its position as the leading high-throughput chain for consumer applications. Meanwhile, Aster Chain's ASTER token appreciated 94% month-over-month following the testnet launch announcement, making it one of the top-performing large-cap assets of the quarter.

Deep Dive: DEX vs CEX — The Shifting Landscape

For the first time since decentralized exchanges began tracking meaningful volume in 2020, the combined spot trading volume of the top five DEXes surpassed 20% of total global crypto spot volume during a single month — April 2026. This milestone reflects structural changes in both user behavior and regulatory environment that are reshaping where digital assets change hands.

Centralized exchange collapses and regulatory enforcement actions across multiple jurisdictions have made self-custody and on-chain settlement increasingly attractive to both retail and institutional traders. Uniswap v4's concentrated liquidity hooks, Curve's cross-chain stablecoin DEX, and the emergence of purpose-built DEX chains like Aster Chain are simultaneously lowering trading costs and improving execution quality to levels that rival mid-tier centralized venues. The remaining gap — primarily in fiat on/off ramps and advanced order types — is narrowing rapidly as bridging infrastructure matures and DEX protocols implement limit order functionality natively.

Institutional adoption of DEX infrastructure remains in early stages but is accelerating. Several prime brokers now offer white-labeled DEX access through compliant smart-contract wrappers that implement AML screening at the application layer while preserving on-chain settlement transparency. This hybrid model is expected to account for a growing share of institutional crypto volume as regulators in major jurisdictions finalize frameworks governing DeFi market structure.
